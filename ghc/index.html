Sobolev spaces
Harbin Engineering University
Kategori : Science

Sobolev spaces, a cornerstone of modern mathematical analysis with profound applications in partial differential equations (PDEs), calculus of variations, and mathematical physics. Classical function spaces—such as continuous or smooth functions—often fall short when modeling real-world phenomena. For instance, solutions to PDEs like the heat equation or wave equation may lack classical derivatives due to irregular data or boundary conditions. To address this, mathematicians introduced weak derivatives, a generalized notion of differentiability that allows differentiation in the sense of distributions. Sobolev spaces, formulated by Sergei Sobolev in the 1930s, integrate these weak derivatives into a functional framework, enabling the study of "almost everywhere" differentiable functions. These spaces are indispensable for developing variational methods, proving existence/uniqueness of PDE solutions, and analyzing their regularity.
